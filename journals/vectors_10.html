The tension here can be understood as a metaphor for the cognitive strain or mental effort required to maintain focus on a particular object, task, or thought.

Attention is a cognitive process that is selective in nature, involving the facilitation of processing certain stimuli while inhibiting others. It requires the brain to allocate resources and can be thought of as a kind of mental work that often demands sustained effort, hence the association with tension. This tension is not just limited to a psychological state but can also manifest as physical strain, such as eye strain or muscle stiffness, particularly during prolonged periods of concentration.

In neuroscience, attention is understood to be associated with various brain networks implicated in executive functions, such as the prefrontal cortex. The 'tension' linked to attention reflects the brain's neurological efforts in coordinating these processes to maintain cognitive focus.

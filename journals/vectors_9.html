These comparisons suggest that consciousness is not fixed but is instead fluid and evolving, characterized by ongoing activity and rhythmic patterns.

A "song" evokes the idea of a temporal sequence with varying tones, suggesting that consciousness has a structure that changes over time, much like a melody that moves through different notes and expressions. A "wave," on the other hand, is a powerful symbol for continuous motion and energy transfer, conjuring images of dynamic oscillation and the natural ebb and flow found in physical systems.

The concept that consciousness might be similar to a song or a wave is innovative and aligns well with current discussions in neuroscience and philosophy of mind, which often describe cognitive processes as emergent patterns within neural networks. The brain does not generate a static 'ghost' concretized within our skulls but hosts a complex dance of neuronal fire, synaptic connections, and biochemical cascades that give rise to the experiential phenomenon we know as consciousness.

The human brain, therefore, is deeply folded - much like the intricate patterns of origami - to increase the surface area where neurons can reside, allowing for greater processing power and functional capabilities within a limited space.

The comparison extends beyond structure to function, as the text suggests that our cerebral cortex acts similarly to a dynamic map of what we know—ever-expanding and adaptable. CBT, meanwhile, is a more established therapeutic approach that helps individuals change patterns of thinking or behavior that are behind people's difficulties, and so change the way they feel.

Overall, the journal page symbolically compares the sophistication of human cognition to the elegance of origami. By doing so, it emphasizes the remarkable adaptability of the brain, which, much like paper in origami, can be reshaped and reconfigured.
